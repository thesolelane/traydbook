### Stan's 10 Common Questions and Suggested Answers

**Q1: What is TraydBook?**
TraydBook is a professional network built exclusively for the construction trades. Contractors build a verified profile, bid on jobs, and get found by project owners. Project owners post RFQs, browse verified contractors, and hire with confidence. Think of it as LinkedIn meets a job board — purpose-built for the trades.

**Q2: Is it really free for contractors?**
Yes. Contractor accounts are always free. No monthly fee, no commission on jobs, no per-lead charge. You sign up, build your profile, and start bidding. You keep 100% of everything you earn.

**Q3: What's the difference between TraydBook and Angi?**
Angi charges contractors $300–$600 a month just to be in the system — and then charges per lead on top of that. The same lead gets sold to multiple contractors, so you're competing before you even pick up the phone. TraydBook charges contractors nothing. You build a profile, get found organically, and submit your own bids. No lead auctions, no shared contact data, no monthly fee.

**Q4: What's the difference between TraydBook and Thumbtack?**
Thumbtack charges $20–$150+ per lead contact, regardless of whether the job converts. TraydBook lets you bid on every open RFQ for free. The project owners who post RFQs have already provided trade, location, budget, and timeline — so you're responding to real, detailed briefs, not cold pay-per-click leads.

**Q5: How long does it take to set up?**
Most contractors are live with a complete profile in 30 minutes or less. You can set up basic info, choose your trade, upload an avatar, and add your verification documents in one sitting.

**Q6: How quickly can I start getting jobs?**
Your profile is visible in search results the moment you finish signup. If there are active RFQs in your trade and location, you can submit your first bid the same day.

**Q7: I'm already busy. Why would I add another platform?**
Because it's free and permanent. You don't have to actively use it every day — your profile works for you in the background. If a project owner searches for your trade in your area, they'll find you. Think of it as a verified professional listing that never costs you anything.

**Q8: What if I don't win any bids?**
Bidding is free, so the risk is zero. You only invest your time in crafting a bid — and every bid you submit is another shot at work. There's no financial downside to submitting bids.

**Q9: Can project owners just spam me?**
No. Project owners who want to message you cold must complete a structured Project Inquiry Form that requires them to provide trade, property type, location, timeline, budget, and a description. It costs them 3 credits to send. That filters out tire-kickers and means any message you receive is from someone with a real project.

**Q10: Do I need to upload my license and insurance?**
You don't have to — but you should. Contractors with the Pro Verified badge (license + GL insurance + workers' comp all verified) appear more prominently in search results and are significantly more likely to win bids and get messages from serious clients. It's a competitive edge that costs you nothing.

### Penny's 10 Common Questions and Suggested Answers

**Q1: What topics should I cover first?**
Start with contractor ROI and platform comparisons. These capture the highest-intent search traffic and directly drive contractor signups. "Why Contractors Are Leaving Angi for TraydBook" would outperform a generic how-to early in the content calendar.

**Q2: How long should articles be?**
Aim for 1,000–1,800 words for most articles. Long enough to cover the topic well and rank well, short enough to stay readable. Deep guides (e.g., license verification, insurance types) can go to 2,500 words if the content warrants it.

**Q3: Should articles be SEO-optimized?**
Yes. Every article should target a primary keyword and 2–3 related terms. Use the keyword naturally in the title, first 100 words, one H2 header, and the conclusion. Don't stuff.

**Q4: Should I include a call to action?**
Always. Every article ends with a CTA — either "Sign up free at traydbook.com" (for contractor-facing content) or "Find a verified contractor at traydbook.com" (for homeowner/investor content).

**Q5: Should I mention competitors by name?**
Yes, in comparison articles. Factual, honest comparisons with Angi, Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor, and Craigslist are valuable for SEO and conversion. State facts, not opinions. "Angi charges contractors $300–$600/month" is fine. "Angi is terrible" is not.

**Q6: Who is the target reader?**
Primarily contractors who want more work without spending on leads. Secondarily, homeowners and investors who want to hire verified professionals. Write for a reader who is smart but not a platform expert.

**Q7: Should articles be written in first person?**
No. Write in the second person ("you") or third person ("contractors who..."). Keep TraydBook as the source and authority, not a first-person narrator.

**Q8: Should articles link to TraydBook features?**
Yes. Always link to the relevant page (e.g., Explore, Bid Board, Verification) when describing those features. Internal links help SEO and guide readers toward signing up.

**Q9: What tone should I use?**
Practical and direct. The reader doesn't want to wade through fluff. Lead with the point, back it up, and end with what the reader should do next.

**Q10: Should I cover bad news or industry problems?**
Yes. Articles that address real problems (the cost of Angi, the skilled trades shortage, getting scammed by unlicensed contractors) build trust and rank well. TraydBook is positioned as the solution, not the problem.

### TraydBook Legal Surface Areas

**1. Contractor Credential Verification Standards**
TraydBook verifies three credential types for the Pro Verified badge:
- Contractor license (issued by the relevant state licensing board)
- General liability insurance certificate (must show active coverage, policy number, and minimum coverage amount)
- Workers' compensation insurance (where legally required by state law for the contractor's trade and business size)

Verification is performed by the TraydBook team. Documents are reviewed for authenticity, expiration dates, and consistency with the contractor's stated trade and location. TraydBook does not guarantee the underlying validity of state-issued licenses — it verifies that a document matching the description was submitted and reviewed. Users who misrepresent their credentials are subject to badge removal and account suspension.

**2. Data Privacy Policy Summary**
TraydBook collects:
- Account information (name, email, trade, location, avatar)
- Usage data (posts, bids, messages, activity on the platform)
- Payment data (processed through Stripe; TraydBook does not store card numbers)
- Verification documents (stored securely; not publicly visible)

TraydBook does not:
- Share user personal data with third parties for advertising
- Sell user data
- Make phone numbers or email addresses visible to other users on the platform

Users may request deletion of their account and associated data by contacting support. Data retention policies apply to transaction records as required by applicable law.

**3. Terms of Service — Key Clauses**
- Users must be 18 or older to create an account
- Users may not misrepresent their trade, credentials, or identity
- Users may not post spam, fraudulent job listings, or fake RFQs
- Contractor accounts are for working tradespeople and licensed contractors — not for lead resellers, aggregators, or bots
- TraydBook reserves the right to remove content or suspend accounts that violate the terms
- Credits are non-refundable except at TraydBook's sole discretion
- TraydBook is not a party to contracts formed between contractors and project owners — any disputes are between the parties involved

**4. DMCA Process for Reported Content**
If a user reports that content on TraydBook (profile photos, portfolio images, posts) infringes their copyright:
1. The reporting party submits a DMCA takedown request with: their identity, the infringing content location, identification of the original work, and a statement of good faith belief
2. Eva receives and logs the request; Linda reviews it
3. If valid, the content is removed and the uploader is notified with a counter-notice option

TraydBook is not a party to contracts formed between contractors and project owners. Any disputes, claims, or liability are between the parties directly. TraydBook's terms of service make clear that the platform is a professional network, not a contractor-client contract facilitator.
