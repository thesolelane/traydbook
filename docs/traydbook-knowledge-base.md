## 1. What is TraydBook?

TraydBook is a professional network built exclusively for the construction trades. It connects licensed contractors, tradespeople, and design professionals with project owners, real estate investors, developers, and homeowners who need their services.

Think of it as LinkedIn meets a job board — purpose-built for the trades. Contractors can post their work, bid on projects, and build a verified reputation. Project owners can post jobs, request bids, and find the right contractor with confidence.

**Website:** traydbook.com
**Tagline:** The professional network built for the construction trades.

## 5. Core Features

### The Feed
The main feed is where the community shares updates, opportunities, and knowledge. All users see the feed after logging in.

**Post types:**
- **Project Update** (blue) — Share progress photos, milestones, or completed work
- **Open Bid** (orange) — Post a project that's actively seeking contractor bids
- **Urgent Hire** (red) — Time-sensitive job openings
- **Trade Tip** (green) — Share knowledge, best practices, safety advice
- **Safety Alert** (yellow) — Warn the community about hazards or site conditions
- **Referral** (purple) — Recommend a contractor to your network

Users can like, comment on, and filter posts by type. The filter bar at the top lets you view only the post types you care about.

---

### Bid Board (RFQ Marketplace)
The Bid Board is where project owners post Requests for Quote (RFQs) and contractors submit competitive bids.

**How it works for project owners:**
1. Post an RFQ describing the project (costs 10 credits)
2. Contractors review and submit bids
3. Owner reviews all bids and awards the contract to their preferred contractor
4. Awarded contractor is notified

**How it works for contractors:**
- Browse open RFQs filtered by trade, location, or budget
- Submit a bid with your price and a message — free, no credits needed
- If awarded, the project is added to your profile history

---

### Job Board
The Job Board is where employers post trade job listings and contractors apply for work.

**Posting a job:**
- Costs 8 credits (for non-contractor accounts)
- Contractors can post jobs free
- Fill out job type, trade, location, pay, and description
- Mark as urgent if time-sensitive

**Applying for a job:**
- Contractors apply directly through the platform for free
- Applicants see job details, pay range, and employer information

---

### Explore (Find a Contractor)
The Explore page is a searchable directory of all contractors on TraydBook.

**Search and filter by:**
- Trade / specialty
- Location (city, state)
- Availability (available now / busy)
- Rating (4.0+, 4.5+, 4.8+)
- Service radius
- Verified badge tier

**Contractor cards show:**
- Name, trade, business name
- Location and years of experience

- Verified badge (if earned)

From here you can view their full profile, connect with them, or start a message thread.

### Messaging
TraydBook has a built-in direct messaging system.

**For contractors messaging anyone:** Free, no restrictions.

- After the first message, the thread becomes free-form chat
- This system filters out tire-kickers and keeps contractors' inboxes serious

**Messages are real-time** — both parties see new messages instantly without refreshing.

## 7. Verified Badge System

TraydBook verifies contractor credentials and awards badges to show clients they can trust who they're hiring.

### Badge tiers

**Pro Verified (orange badge) — highest level**
- Contractor license verified
- General liability insurance verified
- Workers' compensation verified
- All three must be on file and active

**Licensed (blue badge)**
- Contractor license has been verified by TraydBook

**Vouched (green badge)**
- Endorsed by a Pro Verified contractor in good standing
- Pro Verified contractors can vouch for colleagues they personally know and trust

### How to get verified
1. Go to **Settings → Verification & Badges**
2. Upload your license, GL insurance certificate, and/or workers' comp documentation
3. TraydBook reviews your submission
4. Once approved your badge appears on your profile and all your posts

### Why it matters
Verified contractors appear more prominently in search results and are more likely to win bids and get messages from serious project owners.

## 11. Frequently Asked Questions

**Is TraydBook free for contractors?**
Yes. Contractor and trade accounts are always completely free. There are no subscription fees, no listing fees, and no commission taken on jobs or bids.

**How do I get the orange verified badge?**
You need to have your contractor license, general liability insurance, and workers' compensation verified by TraydBook. Go to Settings → Verification & Badges and upload your documents.

**I'm a homeowner. How do I find a contractor?**
Click Explore in the navigation bar. You can search and filter by trade, location, rating, and availability. Click any contractor card to view their full profile and send them a project inquiry.

**How do project inquiries work?**
When you message a contractor for the first time, you fill out a structured Project Inquiry form (trade needed, property type, location, timeline, budget, and project description). This costs 3 credits and ensures the contractor receives everything they need to respond. After that first message the conversation is free-form.

**What happens if I run out of credits?**
You can buy more credits anytime from the Credits page. You'll also be prompted automatically if you try to take an action that requires credits you don't have.

**Can I cancel my SMS subscription?**
Yes. Go to Settings and cancel your SMS alerts subscription anytime. You won't be charged again after cancellation.

**How do I submit a bid on a project?**
Go to the Bid Board, find an open RFQ in your trade, and click Submit Bid. Fill in your price and a message. Submitting bids is always free for contractors.

**Can I use TraydBook on my phone?**
Yes. TraydBook is accessible through any mobile browser. A dedicated mobile app is on the roadmap.

**How does the vouching system work?**
If you are a Pro Verified contractor, you can vouch for other contractors you personally know and trust. Your vouch gives them a green Vouched badge on their profile. Only vouch for people whose work you can genuinely stand behind — your reputation is attached to your endorsements.

**I signed up with Google but want to set a password. Can I do that?**
Yes. Go to Settings → Security and you can add a password to your account.

**How do I report a problem or suspicious account?**
Contact TraydBook support through the contact options on the website. We take fraud and misrepresentation seriously and will investigate all reports.

## 15. What TraydBook Offers That Others Don't — Competitive Differentiation

Most contractor-finding platforms are **lead generation marketplaces**. Their business model is selling your contact information to contractors, often to multiple contractors simultaneously. TraydBook is a **professional network**. The platform earns from project owners (not contractors), and contractors build permanent, verifiable profiles rather than chasing purchased leads.

### Platform-by-platform breakdown

**vs. Angi / HomeAdvisor**
Angi charges contractors $300–$600/month for membership before they even receive a lead. Then each lead carries an additional fee ($15–$85+), and the same lead is sold to multiple contractors simultaneously — meaning you're competing the moment you get the phone number. TraydBook charges contractors nothing. You build a profile, get discovered organically, and submit bids yourself. No lead auctions, no monthly membership, and no shared contact selling.

**vs. Thumbtack**
Thumbtack operates on a per-lead model where contractors pay $20–$150+ per customer contact, regardless of whether the job converts. Leads are frequently low-quality or sent to multiple pros. TraydBook gives you the same access to project owners and job seekers at zero cost — and the structured Project Inquiry system means the leads you do receive already include trade, budget, timeline, and location. Better information, zero cost.

**vs. Craigslist**
Craigslist has no contractor verification, no credential display, no bid system, and no reputation infrastructure. Any anonymous poster can claim to be a licensed contractor. TraydBook has three tiers of verified credentials, a review system, and a professional network designed to make trust visible at a glance.

**vs. LinkedIn**
LinkedIn is a professional network for all industries, with no construction-specific features. There is no RFQ system, no bid board, no trade-specific search, and no credential verification for license or insurance. For a general contractor or tradesperson, LinkedIn offers a generic professional presence — TraydBook offers a purpose-built, trade-verified, opportunity-generating platform.
